About Chase Sheaff: softSkills

Software Engineer and former educator excited to improve education through technology. I was able to set up small groups in my classroom with a edtech app thus allowing more students to get individualized lessons and feedback. Seeing the impressive results led me to join a SaaS edtech startup followed by a coding boot-camp to build my tech skills. I want to bridge my experiences to build and improve edtech apps.

At the EdTech startup I work at, STRUT Learning, I worked on the UX/UI team to design and storyboard our app. When we entered the phase of storyboarding two interns joined our four person design team. We met bi-weekly and consistently set realistic goals to get our designs to developers. This was achieved by stepping up to set clear roles for each person that was most efficient to reach our goal date. Since the interns didn’t know how to use our design tool Figma I assigned them to storyboarding. This not only allowed them to continuously move forward, but allowed designers to make changes to design quickly as interns found flaws or in UX design. Before ending each meeting I reviewed each person’s role so everyone was clear on next steps and exactly how to reach out to everyone if challenges occurred. This resulted in us completing designs and storyboarding within our allotted time frame. Meeting this goal will allow our app to be completed by developers and then meet deadlines for funding sources.
